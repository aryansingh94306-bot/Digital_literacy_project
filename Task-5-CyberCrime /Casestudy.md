Case Study: Digital Identity Theft

1. What is Identity Theft?
Identity theft is a type of cybercrime in which someone illegally gains access to another person’s personal information—such as passwords, ID numbers, or bank details—and misuses it. This can lead to fraud, unauthorized account access, or financial theft carried out in the victim’s name.

2. How It Usually Happens (Step-by-Step)
Consider a typical situation involving a student named Aman:

Initial Trap: Aman receives an email that appears to be from his college’s career services, warning him to complete an “identity verification” to remain eligible for internships.
Fake Website: The email includes a link that directs him to a website that looks exactly like his university’s official login page.
Information Theft: Trusting the page, Aman enters his login details along with sensitive information like his Aadhaar number.
Misuse of Data: The attacker then uses these details to access Aman’s email account. From there, they reset passwords for his banking and social media accounts, gaining complete control over his online identity.

3. Who is Most at Risk?
Students and young professionals are often easy targets for such attacks. They spend a lot of time online and use multiple platforms, but may not always use strong security measures like multi-factor authentication or regular account monitoring.

4. Possible Consequences
The effects of identity theft can be serious. Victims may lose money through unauthorized transactions and suffer long-term financial issues like a damaged credit score. In some cases, they might even face legal trouble if their identity is misused for illegal activities, along with the stress of recovering their accounts.
